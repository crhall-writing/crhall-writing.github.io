it sits across from me  
like an old friend, eyeing  
a bowl of werthers on a coffee table  
my mind wandering into what's capable  

capable of seeing or gleaning  
from such limited experience  
and i like to exist in a periodic orbit  
marcel we called it, me and my therapist  

there is so much i want to forget  
im afraid to be met  
with yet another year  
i fear you'll see the days through my tears  

since im not the one who should be here  
im supposed to care for you and mere  
gaps in age, gasps in breath  
only dig more holes with more depth  

and i see the way you fall alseep  
when you're quiet while i weep  
about what happened before you were born  
or the years that left me nothing but worn  

how could i put that all on you?  
when so much life is to be persued  
and what I can offer are recounts of pain  
and scars and so many staying stains  

and then you tell me  
someone's just waiting, selling  
something hopeful, something splendid  
and nothing works better to sell it  

than a face without crows' feet  
always willing to greet   
future, hope, and all pure and sweet   
existing without my memories  

and it'll always be with me  
in my dying hour I'll meet  
long-past friends, foes, insecurities  
and maybe friends they'll come to be  

but what can i do  
when I can't take care of you?  
when others would be likely to?  
will i ever have a use?
