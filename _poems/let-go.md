title: let go
date: 2026-03-16

“you called me in, boss?”  

“take a seat, we shouldn’t be long”  

and you do, scared of what’s to come  

“I looked at your performance review,”   

she says, eyes stuck on you   

“and I just don’t think you make the cut anymore.”   

your eyebrows furrow and you shift in that chair of yours.   

“but—I’ve been here for even longer than—“   

“look. we’ve got a whole new workforce these days.”   

“I mean—can I ask to what you’re referring?”   

“some of us are words they never got to speak  

even when they knew a person would die.  

they got to speak them, just chose to hide;  

and you want to sit here, ask me why   

you don’t measure up?   

some of us are the body parts   

accidentally cut off   

that have stolen someone’s whole ability   

to talk, to drink, to laugh, to eat, to sleep   

an irreversible mistake causing irreversible shame   

so much that a doctor cannot face them to this day?   

and you want to sit here and claim  

that I should still hold a desk for you all the same?”  

you don’t respond, only stare. she goes on,   

“and where did you ever start to think,  

‘oh, this takes the cake’?  

you are nothing more than days they needed sleep  

so they slept.  

you are nothing more than the times they needed relief  

so they wept.  

you are nothing more than the years of cruelty      

so they left.  

and I’m sorry to say, to such a stable stake  

in our office space, I’ll need you to clear your work surface  

and be gone by the end of the day.”
