title: Vincent Hall
date: 2026-05-07
layout: poem
---
and there was a time  
maybe you remember as well as I    
where you were fuller and bright  
and with some red-haired boy we’d try  

through glides of Hagoromo chalk  
and blackboards grey from overuse  
to find some type of truth, and our walks  
through hallways stained as our youth  

I remember, marked by the light of blonde    
drowned in sun as we left Vincent Hall  
you were so much older than I’d become  
it seemed, as we passed those columns, tall  

and stone, harsh as the reality that’d come between  
a friendship lost to time—lost to seasons  
and snow’d fall on the roof and wind’d scream  
thunder beamed when I attempted to sleep in  

that old house by the train tracks  
humming in the quietest, blaring in the loudest  
times, and I’d wake when matter of fact  
I was always safe, but wondering in my not-proudest  

moments, whether that locomotive was secured    
into my wall, in the middle of the night  
or maybe I was just scared of the future  
and you did always call me the nervous kind  

but these days my hair is darker, and I can tell  
that you touch your roots up more often  
and you’re with someone whose grey hair spells  
the passing of lives, and your ideals softened  

as did mine, and I find myself younger than expected  
at this age, running with those younger and older  
by some significant years, more than anticipated  
and the winters no longer grow colder  

I’m not bundled how we’d once be   
I’m wearing T-shirts in forty degrees
